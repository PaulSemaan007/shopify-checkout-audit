Shopify checkout migration · Diagnostic  

Shopify tells you what you have. Not what you can’t keep.

On 26 August 2026, Shopify replaces the Thank You and Order Status pages on every non-Plus store. Its own upgrade guide will list your Additional Scripts and suggest apps for the ones it recognises — use it, it’s free and it’s good at that job.

What it won’t tell you is which of your scripts cannot be rebuilt at all. Web pixels run in a sandbox with no access to the page, so anything that shows the customer something — a survey, a banner, a chat widget, an A/B test, a trust badge — has no equivalent to migrate to. That is the part that costs real money, and it is the part nothing else flags.

Local only This page makes no network requests. Your scripts are analysed in your browser and are never uploaded, logged, or stored anywhere. You can disconnect from the internet and it still works.

Shopify admin → Settings → Checkout → Additional scripts. The field is read-only now, but you can still select and copy everything in it. Add a row per client store to triage a whole list in one pass.

What this audit is. A static analysis of the scripts you pasted, matched against known tracking vendors and the structural patterns that cannot survive the upgrade. It tells you what is at risk and what replaces it.

What it is not. It cannot see scripts injected by installed apps, and it cannot confirm whether any given tag is currently firing on your live store. Proving that requires watching real orders against real events over time — which is a different job than this page does.
